From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Catalan painter

Emilio (Emi) Grau Sala (1911, Barcelona - 1975, Paris) was a Spanish painter. He studied at the sp:Escuela de Bellas Artes de Barcelona.[1] With his wife Ángeles Santos Torroella he fled to Paris at the outbreak of the Spanish Civil War.[1]

He was a famous colorist (oil painting, watercolor and pastel) and illustrator. In the French Salon "Comparaisons", he was a member of the group of Maurice Boitel.[2]
