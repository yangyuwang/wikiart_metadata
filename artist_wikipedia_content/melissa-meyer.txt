Melissa Meyer (born May 4, 1946) is an American painter.[1] The Wall Street Journal has referred to her as a "lighthearted Abstract Expressionist".[2]

Meyer received a fellowship at the American Academy in Rome in 1980,[3] two National Endowment for the Arts grants (1983, 1993), the National Academy 183rd Invitational Eric Isenburger Annual Award (2008) and a Pollock-Krasner Foundation Grant (2009).[citation needed] In the late 1970s Meyer and Miriam Schapiro collaborated on a Heresies article entitled femmageÂ [fr].[4][5]

In 1997 her sketchbooks were published in facsimile by the Mezzanine Gallery of the Metropolitan Museum of Art.[6]
